01 • PACE

Slow itineraries

I’d rather fully sink into one place than rush through ten. Expect flexible days, long walks, and a “maybe we’ll decide over coffee” energy.

02 • FEELING

Everyday romantic

I’m always chasing scenes that feel cinematic but still real: boat harbors, metro rides at golden hour, and tiny hotel balconies.

Little luxuries

A good view, one really great meal, a comfortable bed, and a playlist. Travel doesn’t have to be complicated to feel special.

Places that shaped my Modern Muse energy

A mix of coastal Europe, city skylines, beach days, and bucket-list views. These are the snapshots that still live rent-free in my head.

Istanbul, Turkey

Divine Culture

A city shaped by centuries of culture and faith. Istanbul seamlessly blends history and modern life, with striking architecture and stories that are layered into every street.

Blue Mosque

Rome, Italy

Postcard streets

Bold, timeless, and rich with history. Rome surrounds you with grand architecture, sculpted stone, and the feeling of walking through living art.

Trevi Fountain

Santorini, Greece

Skyline sunsets

Whitewashed buildings, vivid florals, and endless blue views. Santorini feels slow, romantic, and sun-soaked, where every corner looks effortlessly beautiful.

A Snow White fairytale city

Gold Coast, Australia

Views from the coastline

Watching the light change over the water and city. The calmest thing you'll ever experience

Picture perfect sunsets

Boccadasse, Italy

Afternoons on the rocks

Locals sunbathing on pebbles, kids diving into the water, and the sound of cutlery from seaside restaurants.

Neighborhood beach

New York City, USA

Concrete daydreams

Rooftop views, subway platforms, and the feeling that anything could happen if you just keep walking.

City energy

Hawaii, USA

Saltwater therapy

Sunscreen, salty hair, and staying in the water until your fingers wrinkle.

Beach day

Machu Picchu, Peru

A place full of mystical energy

Clouds moving through the mountains, stone paths, and that weird feeling of standing somewhere you’ve only seen in textbooks.
